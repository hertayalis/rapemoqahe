William Shatner | Net Worth & Income

The Canadian actor, producer, director, and screenwriter with countless hit films and TV shows William Shatner’s net worth of December 2023 is a whopping $100 million.

Astoundingly, the actor’s net worth seems way too low despite the rumors of him bagging massive fortune from his investments.

Here is brief information about what exactly happened:

Over the past few years, there has been a considerable debate on whether or not Mr. Shatner cashed $600 million from Priceline.com.

According to the Chairman and CEO of Priceline Jeffery Boyd himself, the rumor turned out to be a hoax.

Shatner cashed out a decent sum from his stock trading but nothing compared to $600 million.

According to The New York Times article published in October of 2000, for his work in the late 1990s, William was originally awarded 125,000 shares of travel company Priceline.

At the time, the stock seemed like a brilliant move instead of cash, but soon the bubble burst and the company share lost nearly all their value.

At the time, Mr. Shatner sold half of his shares at a maximum price of $104.25, with shares then tanking to $5.56 apiece.

The stocks eventually plunged to $1.80 per share in 2000.

A few years later, Priceline made an astonishing comeback, now trading at $300 per share.

The rumor was that Shatner did not sell any of his stock, leading some to speculate that his shares were now apparently worth more than $600 million.

William has denied the rumors publically on several occasions, particularly in an interview with Howard Stern.

He even took Twitter to handle to deny the claim.

Aside from that, let’s talk about his other income:

The Hollywood veteran has carved out a niche for himself. He has mostly worked on Sci-Fi films and has done pretty well.

In 1997, Shatner starred in a horror Sci-fi film Kingdom of the Spider. He portrayed the role of Rank Hanson, for which he earned $20,000.

Shatner makes around $5 million in a typical year from his several pursuits.

Let’s not forget his most iconic part as Captain Kirk in the Star Trek franchise. For his role, he was paid $10,000 per episode. It sounds way too low but let’s not forget it was back in the 1960s.

William Shatner | TV Shows, Movies, Endorsements, Investments & Books

Movies & TV Shows

Captain Kirk, aka William Shatner, has spent more than 70 years in front of the camera displaying his talent and entertaining people.

He began his career in the theatre.

In 1954, he was cast on the Canadian Howdy Doody Show as Ranger Bob.

He made his feature film debut with The Brothers Karamazov in 1958.

Subsequently, he landed on an episode of The Glass Eye.

Everything aside, his big break came in 1966 with Star Trek. His role as Captain James T. Kirk on the show made him a household name. The show ran from 1966 to 1969.

To this day, the show has a cult following, all thanks to syndicated reruns which made Captain Kirk a cultural icon.

The veteran actor has 247 credits as an actor, including his appearances in films, TV shows, and documentaries.

And surprisingly, even at the age of 91, he is still working. He has three projects in the pipeline with a few waiting to release.

Some of his other popular TV shows include Boston Legal, 3rd Rock From the Sun, A Twist in Tale, TekWar, T.J Hooker, and Ironside.

While some of his most famous films include The Intruder, The Devil’s Rain, Senior Moment, and White Comanche.

William Shatner | Career

William Shatner was born in Montreal, Quebec, Canada, on March 22, 1931, to a Jewish family.

Shatner went to Willingdon Elementary School and West Hill High School.

Similarly, he is an alumnus of Montreal Children’s Theatre.

He graduated in 1952 with a Bachelor’s degree in Commerce.

After college graduation, Shatner worked as the business manager for a playhouse in Canada.

He then enrolled in the Canadian National Repertory Theatre to train as a classical Shakespearean actor.

Moreover, in 2021, Shatner became the oldest person on Earth to fly to space.

He flew to space aboard a Blue Origin sub-orbital capsule along with a Blue Origin executive Audrey Powers, a former Nasa engineer Chris Boshuizen, and CEO of the clinical research firm Glen de Vries.
